Maverick Software helps medical providers collect valuable treatment effectiveness and quality data by providing a software platform to collect clinical and patient-reported outcomes at the point of care and via EHR integration

Improve Patient Safety

Maverick Software allows for collection of powerful metrics at the point of care which allows for evaluation of treatment performance and effectiveness. Numerous academic studies have proven the patient safety related benefits of employing evidence-based medicine practices and Maverick Software provides a streamlined interface for collecting and analyzing clinical and patient reported data without large scale workflow disruptions.

Get The Most Out of EHR Data

Our software works directly with electronic medical record systems to collect clinical and patient reported outcome data from patients. Implementation with EHR systems allows for a streamlined approach to collecting valuable treatment effectiveness metrics without significantly interrupting the workflow at the point of care. Maverick collects and stores only depersonalized data profiles of patients, allowing for secure and quick integration.

Fuel Quality Improvement

Quality improvement initiatives rely on robust and easy to access data. Maverick Software allows for quality improvement personnel to collect real time data at a previously impossible scope and determine the effectiveness of treatments, procedures, and decisions to learn where to focus efforts in quality improvement. Small pieces of data collected with each patient can go a long way in creating a comprehensive quality improvement data context.

Our Story

Maverick Software Systems was officially founded in March 2019, however our story began in January 2018. Co-Founders Joel Seidel and Devanshi Agnihotri created a software solution for tracking medical devices through the supply chain and competed in the 2018 Smeal College of Business Supply Chain Pitch contest, where the team won second place.

In March 2019, Maverick Software Systems was officially founded and was selected to be a part of Invent Penn State's Summer Founders Program and receive funding from the University to get started. As part of this program, the founders decided to pivot from medical device tracking to treatment and procedure quality metrics. The team continues working closely with Penn State University to produce this application and improve the quality initiatives of hospital systems and general practitioners going forward.
